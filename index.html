Game of Thrones Documentation

Season Summaries

Season 1

  • The series begins with the death of Jon Arryn, the Hand of the King.
  • Ned Stark, lord of Winterfell, is appointed as the new Hand and travels to King's Landing with his daughters Sansa and Arya.
  • Daenerys Targaryen, the last surviving member of the deposed Targaryen dynasty, is married off to Khal Drogo.
  • The season ends with Daenerys hatching three dragon eggs.

Season 2

  • The War of the Five Kings begins as different factions vie for control of the Iron Throne.
  • Tyrion Lannister becomes Hand of the King in his father's absence.
  • The Night's Watch faces threats from beyond the Wall, including the White Walkers.
  • Daenerys gains power and an army in Essos.

Season 3

  • The War of the Five Kings continues, with Robb Stark's downfall.
  • Jon Snow infiltrates the Wildlings.
  • Daenerys conquers the city of Astapor and gains an army of Unsullied.
  • The Red Wedding occurs, leading to the deaths of many major characters.

Season 4

  • The Lannisters consolidate power, but face threats from Oberyn Martell and Daenerys Targaryen.
  • Jon Snow and the Night's Watch face danger from the Wildlings.
  • Arya Stark continues her journey and meets the Faceless Men.
  • The season ends with Tyrion Lannister killing his father Tywin and fleeing King's Landing.

Season 5

  • Cersei Lannister's actions lead to her imprisonment by the Faith Militant.
  • Jon Snow becomes Lord Commander of the Night's Watch.
  • Daenerys faces challenges in ruling Meereen.
  • The White Walkers become a greater threat.

Season 6

  • Jon Snow is resurrected and retakes Winterfell from the Boltons.
  • Daenerys returns to Westeros with her dragons.
  • Cersei destroys the Great Sept of Baelor and becomes queen.
  • Bran Stark's visions reveal the truth about Jon Snow's parentage.

Season 7

  • Daenerys and Jon Snow form an alliance to fight the White Walkers.
  • The Night King breaches the Wall with the reanimated dragon Viserion.
  • The season concludes with the Night King and his army advancing south.

Season 8

  • The final season sees the battle against the White Walkers at Winterfell.
  • Cersei Lannister faces off against Daenerys and Jon Snow.
  • The series concludes with the Iron Throne destroyed, Bran Stark becoming king, and various characters' fates revealed.

Character Biographies

Eddard "Ned" Stark

Eddard Stark, commonly known as Ned, is the head of House Stark, Lord of Winterfell, and the father of six children. He is known for his strong sense of honor and duty. Ned becomes the Hand of the King after the previous Hand, Jon Arryn, dies under mysterious circumstances.

Daenerys Targaryen

Daenerys Targaryen is the last surviving member of the Targaryen dynasty, which was overthrown years ago. She is married off to Khal Drogo, a powerful Dothraki warlord, and begins her journey to reclaim the Iron Throne of Westeros. Daenerys is known for her determination and her three dragon companions.

Jon Snow

Jon Snow is the illegitimate son of Eddard Stark. He joins the Night's Watch, a sworn brotherhood tasked with defending the Wall in the North. Jon is known for his strong sense of justice and his leadership skills. His true parentage is a central plot point in the series.

Arya Stark

Arya Stark is the youngest daughter of Eddard Stark and Catelyn Stark. She is known for her strong-willed and independent nature. Arya's journey takes her on a path of revenge as she trains with the Faceless Men to become a skilled assassin.

Tyrion Lannister

Tyrion Lannister, often referred to as the "Imp" or "Halfman," is a member of House Lannister. He is known for his wit, intelligence, and love of books. Despite his family's disdain for him, Tyrion plays a significant role in the politics of Westeros.

Cersei Lannister

Cersei Lannister is a member of House Lannister and the twin sister of Jaime Lannister. She is ambitious and determined to seize power. Cersei's actions lead to significant political upheaval in the Seven Kingdoms.

Jaime Lannister

Jaime Lannister, known as the "Kingslayer," is a member of House Lannister. He begins as a seemingly arrogant and ruthless character but undergoes significant character development throughout the series.

Sansa Stark

Sansa Stark, the eldest Stark daughter, initially dreams of a romantic life at court. She faces numerous hardships and learns the complexities of politics and survival in the world of Westeros.

Bran Stark

Bran Stark, the second youngest Stark, becomes paralyzed after a fall from a tower. He discovers his supernatural abilities, including the power to see into the past and future. Bran's journey takes him beyond the Wall to meet the Three-Eyed Raven.

Robb Stark

Robb Stark, the eldest Stark son, becomes King in the North during the War of the Five Kings. His efforts to seek vengeance for his father's death and secure the North's independence lead to both triumphs and tragedies.

Theon Greyjoy

Theon Greyjoy, a member of House Greyjoy, is taken hostage by the Starks as a child. He struggles to find his place in the world and faces identity conflicts as he tries to balance loyalty to his Stark captors and his Greyjoy heritage.

Samwell Tarly

Samwell Tarly is a member of the Night's Watch and is often ridiculed for his lack of combat skills. However, his intelligence and courage make him a valuable asset in the fight against the White Walkers.

Melisandre

Melisandre, also known as the Red Woman, is a priestess of the Lord of Light. She plays a significant role in the religious and mystical aspects of the series, using her powers for prophecy and magic.

Catelyn Stark

Catelyn Stark is the wife of Eddard Stark and the mother of their children. She is known for her maternal instincts and strong devotion to her family. Her actions and decisions have a significant impact on the events of the series.

Joffrey Baratheon

Joffrey Baratheon, the eldest "son" of King Robert Baratheon, becomes king after Robert's death. He is known for his cruelty and erratic behavior, which leads to tensions and conflicts in King's Landing.

Margaery Tyrell

Margaery Tyrell is a member of House Tyrell and becomes a prominent figure in the political landscape of King's Landing. She marries multiple kings and queens throughout the series, including Renly Baratheon, Joffrey Baratheon, and Tommen Baratheon.

Bronn

Bronn is a skilled sellsword and mercenary known for his wit and combat prowess. He becomes a key ally to various characters, including Tyrion Lannister and Jaime Lannister, throughout the series.

Davos Seaworth

Davos Seaworth, often referred to as the "Onion Knight," is a loyal and honorable servant of Stannis Baratheon. He serves as a trusted adviser and naval commander in Stannis's quest for the Iron Throne.

Tormund Giantsbane

Tormund Giantsbane, often called "Tormund," is a wildling leader known for his strength and humor. He plays a significant role in the alliance between the Night's Watch and the Free Folk against the White Walkers.

Brienne of Tarth

Brienne of Tarth is a formidable warrior who challenges gender stereotypes. She is fiercely loyal and becomes a protector of several key characters, including Catelyn Stark and Sansa Stark.

Grey Worm

Grey Worm is the leader of the Unsullied, an army of highly trained slave soldiers. He is known for his unwavering loyalty to Daenerys Targaryen and his role in her quest for the Iron Throne.

Oberyn Martell

Oberyn Martell, also known as the "Red Viper," is a skilled fighter from Dorne. He seeks vengeance for the death of his sister, Elia Martell, during Robert's Rebellion and becomes embroiled in the politics of King's Landing.

Hodor

Hodor, whose real name is Wylis, is a gentle giant known for his loyalty to Bran Stark. His tragic backstory and the origin of his name are revealed in the series.

Varys, often referred to as the "Spider," is a master of spies and information. He serves as the Master of Whisperers in King's Landing and plays a crucial role in the political intrigues of the Seven Kingdoms.

Ygritte

Ygritte is a wildling woman and skilled archer who becomes Jon Snow's love interest. Her character is known for her fiery spirit and her involvement in Jon's journey beyond the Wall.

Jorah Mormont

Jorah Mormont is an exiled knight who becomes Daenerys Targaryen's loyal adviser and protector. He is known for his deep love for Daenerys and his complicated past.

Gendry

Gendry is a skilled blacksmith and the last known bastard son of King Robert Baratheon. His lineage becomes a significant plot point, and he plays a role in the events of the series.

Missandei

Missandei is a former slave who becomes a trusted adviser and close friend to Daenerys Targaryen. Her character represents the resilience and strength of formerly enslaved individuals.

Stannis Baratheon

Stannis Baratheon is the brother of King Robert Baratheon and claims the Iron Throne as his own. He is known for his strict sense of justice and his determination to rule. His quest for power leads to conflicts and alliances throughout the series.

Shireen Baratheon

Shireen Baratheon is the daughter of Stannis Baratheon and Selyse Baratheon. She is known for her kind and bookish nature. Her tragic fate becomes a significant storyline in the series.

Osha

Osha is a wildling woman who becomes a protector and caregiver for Bran and Rickon Stark. Her character is known for her resourcefulness and survival skills.

Maester Aemon

Maester Aemon is a wise and elderly maester of the Night's Watch. He provides guidance and counsel to Jon Snow and other members of the Night's Watch throughout the series.

High Sparrow

The High Sparrow is the leader of the Faith Militant and plays a significant role in the religious and political turmoil in King's Landing. His rise to power has far-reaching consequences for the characters in the series.

Westeros and Essos Maps

Noble Houses and Lineages

House Stark

House Stark of Winterfell is one of the Great Houses of Westeros and is known for its ancestral home, Winterfell. The Starks are known for their loyalty, honor, and the motto "Winter is Coming." They have a rich lineage that includes figures like Eddard Stark, Catelyn Stark, and their children, including Jon Snow.

House Lannister

House Lannister of Casterly Rock is one of the wealthiest and most powerful houses in Westeros. They are known for their cunning, wealth, and the motto "Hear Me Roar!" Key members include Tywin Lannister, Cersei Lannister, Jaime Lannister, and Tyrion Lannister.

House Targaryen

House Targaryen is a formerly ruling dynasty known for its connection to dragons and the motto "Fire and Blood." Key Targaryen figures include Daenerys Targaryen, Aerys II Targaryen (the Mad King), and Rhaegar Targaryen.

House Baratheon

House Baratheon is known for its claim to the Iron Throne. Key members include Robert Baratheon, Stannis Baratheon, and Renly Baratheon. Their house motto is "Ours is the Fury."

House Tully

House Tully of Riverrun is known for its connections to rivers and waterways. Notable Tullys include Catelyn Tully Stark and Edmure Tully. Their motto is "Family, Duty, Honor."

House Frey

House Frey is known for its vast numbers and the infamous Red Wedding. Key Freys include Walder Frey and his numerous offspring. Their motto is "We Stand Together."

House Greyjoy

House Greyjoy of Pyke is known for its seafaring culture and reaving. Notable Greyjoys include Balon Greyjoy, Theon Greyjoy, and Yara Greyjoy. Their motto is "We Do Not Sow."

House Martell

House Martell of Sunspear hails from the region of Dorne and is known for its distinct culture. Key Martell figures include Oberyn Martell and Doran Martell. Their motto is "Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken."

House Tyrell

House Tyrell of Highgarden is known for its wealth and agricultural prowess. Prominent Tyrells include Margaery Tyrell and Olenna Tyrell. Their motto is "Growing Strong."

House Arryn

House Arryn of the Eyrie is known for its mountainous stronghold. Key Arryn figures include Jon Arryn and Lysa Arryn. Their motto is "As High as Honor."

House Bolton

House Bolton is infamous for its cruelty and flayed man sigil. Roose Bolton and Ramsay Bolton are prominent members. Their motto is "Our Blades Are Sharp."

House Mormont

House Mormont of Bear Island is known for its fierce and loyal warriors. Notable Mormonts include Jeor Mormont and Lyanna Mormont. Their motto is "Here We Stand."

House Clegane

House Clegane is known for its formidable warriors, Sandor "The Hound" Clegane and Gregor "The Mountain" Clegane. Their house sigil features three dogs. They have no official motto.

House Tarly

House Tarly of Horn Hill is known for its skilled soldiers and loyalty. Notable Tarlys include Randyll Tarly and Samwell Tarly. Their motto is "First in Battle."

House Reed

House Reed of Greywater Watch is known for its mysterious and secretive nature. Prominent Reeds include Jojen Reed and Meera Reed, who assist Bran Stark in his journey. They have no official motto.

House Karstark

House Karstark is a vassal house of the Starks of Winterfell. Notable Karstarks include Rickard Karstark and Alys Karstark. Their motto is "The Sun of Winter."

Weapons and Artifacts

Valyrian Steel Swords

Valyrian steel swords are rare and prized weapons known for their sharpness and effectiveness against White Walkers. Notable swords include Ice (House Stark), Longclaw (House Mormont), and Oathkeeper (House Tarly).

Dragonglass

Dragonglass, also known as obsidian, is a volcanic glass used to make weapons effective against White Walkers. It is found in limited quantities and plays a crucial role in the defense against the White Walkers.

Iron Throne

The Iron Throne is the symbolic seat of power in the Seven Kingdoms, located in the Red Keep of King's Landing. It is forged from the swords of Aegon the Conqueror's enemies and is highly coveted by rulers throughout the series.

Three-Eyed Raven's Cave

The Three-Eyed Raven's Cave is a mystical location beyond the Wall, where Bran Stark undergoes training to become the new Three-Eyed Raven. It is a place of ancient knowledge and magical significance.

The Crown of the Iron Islands

The Crown of the Iron Islands is a significant artifact in the culture of the ironborn. It represents the rulership of the Iron Islands and is worn by the leader of House Greyjoy.

The Hound's Helmet

The Hound's helmet is a distinctive piece of headgear worn by Sandor Clegane, also known as "The Hound." It features a gruesome face design and is a symbol of his fearsome reputation.

The Iron Fleet

The Iron Fleet is a formidable naval force of House Greyjoy, known for its ironborn longships. It is a key asset for House Greyjoy in their raids and maritime endeavors.

The Hand of the King Pin

The Hand of the King pin is a symbol of the Hand's role as the chief adviser to the ruler of the Seven Kingdoms. It is a prestigious and coveted position in the realm.

The Red Woman's Necklace

The Red Woman's necklace is a magical artifact worn by Melisandre, also known as the "Red Woman." It is associated with her mystical powers and serves as a focal point for her rituals.

The Night King's Spear

The Night King's spear is a deadly weapon wielded by the leader of the White Walkers. It is used in their icy assaults on the living and poses a significant threat.

The Long Night

"The Long Night" is a legendary sword from ancient tales, said to have been forged from a fallen star. It is associated with the hero Azor Ahai and the prophecy of the Prince That Was Promised.

The Harpy's Mask

The Harpy's Mask is a symbol of the Sons of the Harpy, a resistance group in Slaver's Bay. It represents their opposition to the rule of Daenerys Targaryen in Meereen.

The Horn of Joramun

The Horn of Joramun is a legendary artifact said to have the power to bring down the Wall, the massive ice structure separating the Seven Kingdoms from the lands beyond.

The Red Viper's Poison

The Red Viper's poison refers to the deadly toxins used by Oberyn Martell, the "Red Viper," in his duel against Gregor Clegane. It highlights his skill in poisoncraft.

Creatures and Mythical Beings

Dragons

Dragons are majestic, fire-breathing creatures of immense power. They are associated with House Targaryen and are central to the series. Notable dragons include Drogon, Rhaegal, and Viserion.

White Walkers

White Walkers, also known as the Others, are supernatural beings that pose a grave threat to the living. They are known for their icy appearance and ability to reanimate the dead.

Direwolves

Direwolves are large and intelligent wolves that are closely associated with House Stark. Each Stark child has a direwolf companion, and their fates are intertwined.

Giants

Giants are enormous humanoid creatures who inhabit the lands beyond the Wall. They are allies of the Free Folk and play a role in the defense against the White Walkers.

Children of the Forest

The Children of the Forest are a mysterious and ancient race with powerful magical abilities. They are known for their connection to the weirwood trees and their role in history.

Wights

Wights are reanimated corpses raised by the White Walkers. They serve as foot soldiers in the White Walkers' armies and are impervious to most conventional weapons.

Krakens

Krakens are enormous sea creatures rumored to inhabit the waters around the Iron Islands. They are a part of the folklore of House Greyjoy.

Shadowbinders

Shadowbinders are practitioners of shadow magic, as seen with Melisandre. They have the ability to manipulate shadows and use them for various purposes, including assassination.

The Great Other

The Great Other is a deity-like figure in the religion of R'hllor (the Lord of Light). It is often contrasted with R'hllor and represents darkness and cold.

Ice Spiders

Ice spiders are mythical creatures associated with the White Walkers. While they are rarely seen in the series, they are part of White Walker lore.

Wargs

Wargs are individuals with the ability to enter the minds of animals and control them. Bran Stark and Jon Snow both exhibit warg abilities with their direwolves.

The Great Stallion

The Great Stallion is a deity worshipped by the Dothraki. It is often invoked by the Dothraki riders and plays a significant role in their culture and beliefs.

Greyscale Afflicted

Greyscale is a contagious and disfiguring disease. Those afflicted with it are often shunned by society and can become outcasts in Westeros and Essos.

Highlighted Episodes

Season 1, Ep 1 - "Winter Is Coming"

The series begins with the death of Jon Arryn, setting in motion a chain of events that lead to Ned Stark becoming the Hand of the King. Meanwhile, Daenerys Targaryen is married off to Khal Drogo, and the season ends with her hatching three dragon eggs.

Season 1, Ep 9 - "Baelor"

In this pivotal episode, Ned Stark's fate is decided as he faces the judgment of the Iron Throne. A shocking turn of events has far-reaching consequences for the Stark family and the realm.

Season 3, Ep 9 - "The Rains of Castamere" (The Red Wedding)

Infamous as the "Red Wedding" episode, "The Rains of Castamere" features a shocking and brutal event that has become a defining moment in television history. House Stark, attending a seemingly peaceful wedding, faces a devastating betrayal orchestrated by House Frey and House Bolton. The episode is known for its heart-wrenching and intense scenes, marking a turning point in the power dynamics of Westeros.

Season 6, Ep 9 - "Battle of the Bastards"

This episode showcases an epic and brutal battle between Jon Snow and Ramsay Bolton for control of Winterfell. It's known for its stunning cinematography and intense action sequences.

Season 8, Ep 6 - "The Iron Throne" (Series Finale)

The series finale brings closure to the story of the Iron Throne. The fate of key characters is decided as the struggle for power comes to a dramatic conclusion.
